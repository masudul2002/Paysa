# Executive Summary

Paysa is intended to become a production-grade personal finance product that prioritizes offline reliability, financial clarity, and long-term maintainability.
This PRD defines the business foundation for the product and establishes the scope for future product and engineering decisions.

# Product Vision

Create a trusted personal finance experience that helps users understand, organize, and control their money with confidence.

# Product Mission

Deliver a disciplined product definition that supports a stable, scalable, and documentation-driven path from foundation to release.

# Problem Statement

Individuals need a dependable way to manage personal finances without depending on fragmented tools, inconsistent workflows, or network availability.
The product must reduce friction while preserving user trust and data clarity.

# Business Goals

- Help users manage personal finances with confidence and consistency.
- Establish a stable product foundation for long-term growth.
- Support future expansion without forcing repeated redesigns.
- Maintain strong trust through clarity, reliability, and privacy-minded decisions.

# Success Metrics

- User workflows can be completed without unnecessary steps or confusion.
- Core finance activities remain available in offline scenarios.
- Product and documentation stay aligned across releases.
- The roadmap can expand without destabilizing the foundation.

# Project Objectives

- Define the business scope of the product.
- Establish shared understanding across product, engineering, QA, and design.
- Keep future decisions traceable to documented requirements.
- Separate product intent from technical implementation choices.

# Non Objectives

- Do not define implementation details in this document.
- Do not specify Flutter-level architecture or widget behavior here.
- Do not commit to delivery dates that have not been approved.
- Do not predefine future sync mechanics beyond business intent.

# Product Scope

The product scope covers personal finance management capabilities that support daily tracking, visibility, and planning.
Specific functional capabilities will be detailed in later phases.

# Out of Scope

- Complex enterprise accounting workflows.
- Unsupported third-party integrations.
- Unapproved cloud synchronization behavior.
- Implementation-specific technical decisions.

# Assumptions

- The product will begin with an offline-first foundation.
- Users value privacy, clarity, and local reliability.
- Future product phases may expand the scope of synchronization.
- Some detailed product decisions remain to be defined in later phases.

# Constraints

- The product must remain implementation-independent at this stage.
- The product must not rely on network availability for core value delivery.
- Regulatory, regional, and market-specific details are to be defined in later phases.
- Any future expansion must preserve maintainability and reviewability.

# Stakeholders

- Product Management: owns business scope and prioritization.
- Engineering: owns feasibility review and delivery execution.
- QA: owns validation and requirement traceability.
- Design: owns usability and experience quality.
- Future users: provide feedback through product testing and release cycles.

# User Personas (overview only)

- Individual budget planner: wants visibility into spending and savings.
- Daily expense tracker: wants quick, reliable transaction recording.
- Long-term planner: wants patterns and summaries over time.
- Future power user: wants scalable workflows and broader financial insight.

# Risks

- Scope expansion may slow delivery if not controlled.
- Ambiguous requirements may create inconsistent product decisions.
- Future sync planning may create unnecessary complexity if introduced too early.
- Documentation drift may reduce trust and implementation alignment.

# Dependencies

- Agreement on the product scope and roadmap.
- Ongoing documentation discipline.
- Cross-functional review of requirements before implementation.
- Some integration and platform details are to be defined in later phases.

# High-Level Milestones

- Repository foundation and product definition.
- Core offline finance workflows.
- Reporting and planning baseline.
- Future sync readiness assessment.
- Release preparation and stabilization.

# Glossary

- Offline-first: the product remains useful without network access.
- Personal finance manager: a product for tracking, understanding, and planning personal money usage.
- Future sync: a later-phase capability for secure multi-device data consistency.
- MVP: the minimum viable product scope approved for initial delivery.
